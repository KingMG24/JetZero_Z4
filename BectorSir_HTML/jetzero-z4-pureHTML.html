JetZero Z4 — Blended Wing Body Aircraft

Redefining Commercial Aviation

The JetZero Z4 is not an iteration — it is a reinvention. By integrating the passenger cabin directly into a lifting-body airframe, the Z4 eliminates the aerodynamic compromise inherent in every aircraft flying today. The result is an aircraft that carries 250 passengers with 50% less fuel than conventional tube-and-wing designs.

Backed by the U.S. Air Force and a growing roster of commercial airline partners, JetZero is on track for first flight with its full-scale demonstrator before the end of the decade. Entry into commercial service is projected for approximately 2030.

Key headline figures: 250 passengers | 5,000+ nautical miles range | 50% fuel reduction per seat | 100% SAF compatible

Engineering & Technology

The Blended Wing Body Advantage

Conventional aircraft carry their fuselage as dead weight — a cylindrical tube that generates drag but contributes nothing to lift. The Z4's blended wing body eliminates this trade-off entirely. Every square metre of the airframe generates lift, slashing the energy required per seat by a margin impossible to achieve through incremental engine or material improvements alone.

Propulsion

Key Features

A summary of the Z4's defining capabilities across performance, sustainability, passenger experience, and operator economics:

  • Blended Wing Body eliminates parasitic fuselage drag
  • 50% fuel reduction versus best-in-class narrow-body
  • 100% drop-in Sustainable Aviation Fuel compatible
  • Hydrogen propulsion upgrade path built in from day one
  • Dorsal engine placement provides full airframe noise shielding
  • Quad-redundant fly-by-wire flight control system
  • Distributed elevon control surfaces for enhanced handling
  • Carbon-fibre composite primary structure — 30% lighter than aluminium
  • Multi-lobe pressure vessel design meets FAA Part 25
  • 250-passenger wide cabin with multiple seating layout options
  • Lower cabin altitude equivalent (6,000 ft) versus conventional jets
  • 5,000+ nmi range enabling direct point-to-point long-haul routes
  • 40–50% reduction in Cost per Available Seat Mile (CASM)
  • Compatible with existing Category E airport gate infrastructure
  • Modular systems architecture for rapid MRO turnaround
  • U.S. Air Force tanker variant in parallel development
