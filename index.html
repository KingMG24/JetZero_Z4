About the Z4

Redefining Commercial Aviation

The JetZero Z4 is not an iteration; it is a reinvention. By integrating the passenger cabin directly into a lifting-body airframe, the Z4 eliminates the aerodynamic compromise inherent in every aircraft flying today.

The Blended Wing Body Advantage

Conventional aircraft carry their fuselage as dead weight: a cylindrical tube that generates drag but contributes nothing to lift. The Z4's blended wing body eliminates this trade-off entirely. Every square meter of the airframe generates lift, slashing the energy required per seat by a margin that cannot be achieved with incremental engine or material improvements alone.

Backed by the U.S. Air Force and a growing roster of commercial airline partners, JetZero is on track for first flight with its full-scale demonstrator before the end of the decade.

✈

Aerodynamic Efficiency

The continuous lifting surface achieves a lift-to-drag ratio 50% higher than the best narrow-body aircraft in service today, translating directly into lower fuel burn and reduced operating costs.

🌿

Net-Zero Pathway

100% compatible with Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) from day one. The Z4's structural efficiency means the entire industry could meet 2050 net-zero targets without waiting for new propulsion technology.

👤

Passenger Experience

A wide, panoramic cabin with multiple aisle configurations gives passengers more room, reduced cabin altitude pressure, and lower noise levels than any tube-and-wing aircraft of comparable capacity.

🛠

Proven Technology Base

Advanced composite materials and modular systems drawn from both aerospace-grade military programs and the latest commercial aviation supply chain ensure reliability and ease of maintenance.

📊

Operating Economics

Lower fuel burn translates to a 40–50% reduction in CASM (Cost per Available Seat Mile) versus comparable aircraft, a game-changing advantage for any airline operating the Z4.

🌎

Global Range

A range exceeding 5,000 nautical miles positions the Z4 to serve transatlantic, transpacific, and ultra-long-haul point-to-point routes that today require inefficient connecting hubs.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked

A Blended Wing Body (BWB) merges the fuselage and wings into a single, continuous lifting surface. Unlike a conventional tube-and-wing design where most lift comes from the wings, a BWB generates lift across its entire span, dramatically reducing drag, structural weight, and fuel consumption.
JetZero targets a first flight demonstration in approximately 2027, with a path toward commercial entry into service in the early 2030s, subject to FAA type certification milestones.
The 50% fuel reduction versus a Boeing 777-equivalent stems from three compounding factors: a lift-to-drag ratio roughly 50% higher than tube-and-wing aircraft, a structurally lighter airframe due to load distribution across the entire wing, and full compatibility with 100% Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF).
Yes. The Z4 is designed to fit within an ICAO Code E gate footprint (the same category as the Boeing 777 and Airbus A350), allowing it to operate from any major international airport without infrastructure modifications.
Sustainable Aviation Fuel is produced from non-fossil feedstocks such as agricultural waste, municipal solid waste, or captured CO₂. It can reduce lifecycle carbon emissions by up to 80% versus conventional jet fuel and is a drop-in replacement requiring no engine modifications. The Z4 is 100% SAF-compatible from day one.
The wide, distributed cabin enables multiple aisle configurations supporting premium, business, and economy seating for up to 250 passengers. The broader fuselage also reduces cabin altitude pressure differential, improving passenger comfort on long-haul flights.
